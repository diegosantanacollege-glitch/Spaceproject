Explore the solar system

  • The Sun (Radius: 432,690 miles)

    92.96 million miles from earth

    The Sun is our star that helps hold everything together in our solar system. With its high gravity the Earth stays in rotation. The Sun evokes a type of radiation called solar radiation. Solar Radiation allows Earth to have heat,light and energy to funnel life on Earth. Fun Fact: It takes about 8 minutes for sunlight to hit Earth!

  • Mercury (Radius: 1516 miles)

    36 million miles from earth

    Mercury is the closest planet to the Sun. It is also the smallest planet in the entire solar system. The surface of this planet is close to that of the Earths moon. Mercurys atmosphere is also relatively thin. Mercury can rotate across the entire sun in just 88 days. Fun Fact: Mercury has a day longer than its year. With a full rotation around the sun in 88 days but a full spin on its axis being 176 days.

  • Venus (Radius: 3760.5 miles)

    24 million miles from earth

    Venus is the hottest planet in the solar system because its atmosphere traps more heat inside. It is also the second closest to the sun and closest to Earth. Venus has a rotation along the sun in 225 days. While a day and night would last 243 days Fun fact: Venus has no moon but it has a quasi-satellite called Zoozve.

  • Earth (Radius: 3936 miles)

    Our home planet!

    Earth is our home planet! It is the only planet in our solar system to actually support life. Its able to support life because of the access of water in this planet. Earth has a day of 24 hours with a year in 365 days. Earth has one Moon and billions of unique lifeforms! Fun fact: Earths core is as hot as the surface of the Sun.

  • Mars(Radius: 2,106 miles)

    33.9 million miles from earth

    Mars is one of the most reserched planets in our solar system. Its proximity to Earth makes it one of the most talked about planets in our solar system. Nasa and other agencies have continued to drop rovers and satellites to search for signs of life. Mars is also very close to the size and gravity of earth. Yet, a year on mars last 687 days while one day last 24.6 hours. Fun fact: Mars has 2 moons. (Phobos and Deimos)

  • Jupiter (Radius: 43.440.7 miles)

    444.7 million miles from earth

    Jupiter is the biggest planet in our solar system. Jupiter has a day of just 9.9 hours and a year of 12 earth years. Jupiter has around 95 moons. Fun fact: Jupiter has rings! However, they are extremely difficult to see.

  • Saturn (Radius: 37,448.5 miles)

    746 million miles from earth

    Saturn is the second largest planet in our solar system. However, it is well known for its famous rings. These rings are caused by the gravitational pull of Saturn and the debris that are locked inside. Saturn has a year of 29 earth years with a day lasting only 10.7 hours. Fun fact: Saturn has 274 moons.

  • Uranus (Radius: 15,859.1 miles)

    1.6 billion years from earth

    Uranus was the first planet noticed by a telescope in 1781. Uranus has a rotation of 17 hours with a full sun rotation of 84 earth years. Uranus has 28 moons. Most of the plant is made of icy materials such as water,methane and ammonia. Fun fact: Uranus also has a ring system! One ring system is gray while the other ring system is blue.

  • Neptune (Radius: 15,759.1 miles)

    2.8 billion years from earth

    Neptune is the last planet in our solar system. It is the farthest planet from the sun. Neptune has a day of 16 hours and a year of 165 earth years. Neptune has 16 moons. Fun fact: Neptune's nickname is called the "Blue Giant" because of its famous blue color.
